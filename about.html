Structured Intelligence for Real-World Automotive Faults

The Problem

Modern vehicles are complex systems. When something fails whether it’s a fault code, an electrical issue, a mechanical component or a single door that suddenly stops locking, diagnosis may rely on scattered forum posts, anecdotal advice, and repeated trial-and-error.

Valuable real-world experience exists but it is fragmented across thousands of disconnected sources and rarely structured in a way that reveals meaningful patterns.

The Vision

FaultNet aims to build a structured, searchable intelligence network of real-world automotive faults that is based on realiable experience and data from both drivers and technicians.

By collecting and organizing fault cases across vehicles, environments, mileage, symptoms, and repair outcomes, FaultNet seeks to surface patterns that are often missed when problems are viewed in isolation.

What FaultNet Covers

  • OBD and manufacturer-specific fault codes
  • Mechanical failures
  • Electrical faults
  • Intermittent or environment-triggered issues
  • Single-component failures (e.g., one door lock stops working)
  • Recurring model-specific weaknesses

Why It Matters

Diagnosis becomes easier when patterns are visible.

Instead of relying solely on guesswork or isolated forum threads, FaultNet aims to provide structured insights that help drivers and technicians make more informed decisions.
